Dance Program

Pushpanjali

Nattai / Adi / Traditional

An invocatory piece offering flowers to the deity, seeking blessings before the commencement of the performance. The dancer pays obeisance to the stage, the audience, and the musicians.

Alarippu

Misra Chapu / Traditional

A dance sequence showcasing the gradual awakening of the dancer's body. Beginning with the eyes, then neck, shoulders, and finally ending with intricate rhythmic footwork.

Jathiswaram

Arabhi / Adi / Mysore Vasudevacharya

A pure rhythmic dance consisting of a harmonious blend of jathis (rhythmic patterns) and swaras (musical notes).

Shabdam

Khamboji / Misra Chapu / Traditional

A piece that combines abhinaya (expressive elements) with nritta (pure dance), introducing the storytelling aspect of Bharatanatyam.

Dance Program (continued)

Varnam

Shanmukhapriya / Adi / Papanasam Sivan

Varnam (color), as the name suggests, is the centerpiece of a Bharatanatyam repertoire. It consists of challenging choreography, requiring intricate rhythmic footwork and refined abhinaya (expressive elements), allowing the dancer to showcase technical and artistic refinements.

Keerthanam

Chakravakam / Kanda Ekam / Gopalakrishna Bharathi

A nritya (expressive) piece that is descriptive in nature, where the dancer weaves a story with her gestures and facial expressions. This Keerthanam is in praise of Lord Nataraja of Chidambaram.

Padam

Suruti / Roopakam / Marimutha Pillai

A pure nritya piece tests the dancer's ability to emote as another character. "Why are you so angry with me?" asks the sarcastic heroine in this padham.

Tillana

Sindalam / Kanda Ekam / Veenai Krishnamachariar

Performed towards the finale, Tillana is the epitome of pure rhythmic artistry. The choreography demands perfect execution of footwork and body movements.

Mangalam

A thankful prayer and conclusion to the recital.
